Tandoori Chicken

Tandoori chicken is a special recipe from South East Asian where the chicken is marinated overnight in a special mixture of spices and then cooked in a special oven called the Tandoor. However, for our purposes any source of heat is fine. There is debate is to when this recipe originated from but some sources say it originated from the Mughal period.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 kg of chicken
  • 1/2 cup of Greek yogurt
  • 3/4 tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/4 tsp crushed black pepper powder
  • 1 tsp of coriander powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt (Add more as desired)
  • 1/4 tsp tumeric
  • 1 tsp of kasuri methi
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 and 1/2 tbsp mustard oil

For Grilling

  • 1 tbsp oil

For Color

  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 to 2 tbsp mustard oil

Steps

Marinade for Tandoori Chicken

  1. To a mixing bowl, add greek yogurt or hung curd (thick curd). If you do not have it make your own following the notes below.
  2. Next, add in ginger garlic paste, garam masala, red chilli powder, salt, tumeric, kasuri methi, pepper powder and coriander powder.
  3. Pour oil and lemon juice as well.
  4. Mix everthing well. The marinade has to be thick and not of dripping consistency. Taste it and add more salt and chilli powder if needed.
  5. Make deep gashes over the chicken and add it to the bowl
  6. Marinate the chicken well into the gashes.
  7. Cover and set aside for at least 6 hours. If you reduce the marination time, the chicken will not turn soft and juicy

How to Make Tandoori Chicken

  1. Preheat the oven to the highest for at least 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. Place a foil on a oven tray to collect the drippings
  3. Then keep a greased wire rack on the tray
  4. Place the chicken pieces over the wire rack
  5. Grill the chicken for about 15 minutes
  6. For a spicy version mix together 1 tsp of red chilli powder with 1 tbsp of oil
  7. After 15 minutes of grilling, baste the red chili oil over the chicken. You can also baste the leftover marinade over the chicken.
  8. Turn the chicken to the other side and baste the red chili oil or marinade
  9. Grill for another 6 to 10 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through. You will need to adjust the timing as it depends on the size of the chicken pieces.
  10. The last 5 minutes set to broil mode.
  11. Brush the drippings all over the chicken
  12. Serve with Cilantro chutney alongside onion and/or lemon wedges
